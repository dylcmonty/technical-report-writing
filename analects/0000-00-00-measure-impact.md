# Measure Impact

I think the impact that matters is twofold, without forcing it into a crystallized or overly rigid metric system.
The first part is intentionality. It is not fully measurable, yet it can still be sensed, even when it is expressed differently across people and communities.
Someone once said, "We are all born, but at some point we choose to be human." That idea can be dangerous if misused, but it still points at something real.
The second part is living in a way that rewards that intentionality: seeking out people and missions that embody it.
Part of this means devoting our lives to what we see is missing in the world, without claiming we can "save the world." That claim collapses into nihilism. But if we trust that goodness is larger than any one person, and that others are also carrying that torch, then we can work with humility. We should give up trying to control the world and still pursue what is good, even while knowing our limits.
