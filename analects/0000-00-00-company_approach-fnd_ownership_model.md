# FND Ownership Model

Question: What is FND's stance on client ownership, and why does it matter?

Answer:
FND's operating model centers on retained ownership. Clients keep control of their domains, payment accounts, and mailboxes; FND operates through scoped implementation access rather than permanent control. Current services include website hosting and development, domain and email integration, payment and POS setup, onboarding, analytics, reporting extensions, and optional hardware and instrumentation support. Hardware and software are open source where control, portability, and interoperability matter. FND matches common competitor pricing for basic hosting while transparently passing through processor fees, so the transition to the network costs no more than maintaining the status quo. The intended outcome is dependable local provisioning that improves income stability, reduces waste, and preserves local control rather than deepening vendor lock-in. FND emphasizes incremental rollout and replaceable components, with no forced platform migration.

Boundaries:
- This is a model for the current service layer; the longer-range coordination network is a separate phase.
- No-lock-in is a design constraint, not merely a marketing claim: it shapes implementation decisions.
