# Competitive positioning

FND competes around farm management software, local food marketplaces, food-hub platforms, ecommerce tools, POS systems, and generic tools such as Shopify, Square, QuickBooks, and spreadsheets.

Most alternatives either sell software to farms or build a closed marketplace. Both approaches can struggle when farms are asked to adopt tools or surrender data before receiving enough immediate value.

FND’s position is different. The farmer-side tool is the adoption mechanism, not the main monetization point. The buyer-side procurement problem is where the stronger commercial pain is. Farmers get tools that help them operate and expose supply; buyers pay for brokerage, coordination, and reliable access to local product.
