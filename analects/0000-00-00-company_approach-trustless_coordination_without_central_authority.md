# Trustless Coordination Without Central Authority

Question: Why is decentralized coordination a requirement rather than a preference?

Answer:
The approach assumes that local entities must self-organize while preserving autonomy and fiscal self-preservation. A central authority can coordinate by force, but it also becomes a bottleneck and gatekeeper over participation rules and data visibility. The intended model is trust-minimized coordination where participants can interoperate, exchange useful signals, and preserve local control without surrendering operational independence to one platform owner.

Boundaries:
- This does not claim governance is unnecessary.
- This does not claim all centralized tools are ineffective in every context.
