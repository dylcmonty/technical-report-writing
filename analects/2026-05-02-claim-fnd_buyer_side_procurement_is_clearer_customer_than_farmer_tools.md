# Buyer-side procurement is the clearer commercial customer

The clearer commercial customer for FND is the recurring local-food buyer, not the individual farm software user.

Farms are necessary supply-side participants, but many farms have limited software budgets and uncertain willingness to pay for another management tool. Grocers, food hubs, institutions, and other recurring buyers have a more concentrated procurement problem: they need reliable local supply at usable price, timing, volume, and service levels.

This distinction changes the business model. FND can support farms with free or low-cost tools while monetizing the buyer-side layer through brokerage, procurement coordination, transaction fees, service contracts, or logistics coordination.

The company therefore should not be reduced to farm software. The stronger business frame is a coordination and brokerage layer that uses farmer software to create supply-side participation.
