# Literary Morning Passage

## Retrieval Gist

Short literary passage about the strange interior world of early morning before plans and obligations harden into the day.

## Use When

Use when retrieving poetic writing about memory, time, morning consciousness, or the feeling of slipping outside planned life.

## Text

Waking up early,
Before there were days planned,
Those were bubbles of a life lived, 
Different from what's real now,
Different than the mornings known now,
That wasn't a time of day, it was a world that got snuck into. 
I don't remember leaving that world but I suppose eventually days were had after the sun broke.
But now I remember my mornings, I remember my plans from the morning and for that morning.

I'm here awake before the sun, and I find my self back in that world. I must have slipped into this world when I forgot was next. I don't have a plan. It's just this secret world I accidentally snuck into, nothing else. The early morning place before days were planned.

January 16
