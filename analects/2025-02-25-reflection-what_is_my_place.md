# 02/05/25

## Retrieval Gist

Reflection on vocation, artistry, writing, technology, and the desire to build tools that help people feel seen, empowered, and able to create.

## Use When

Use when retrieving personal writing about artistic identity, the tension between expression and infrastructure, or the motive for building enabling tools.

## Text

I see myself as an artist. I think that this means I have a deep desire to be understood and a deep lack of feeling like I am. I love to create. I question, though, what craft do I hone and what creation do I byde time with. I’ve always loved to write, I love to paint, I love to garden, I love to wood work, to draw and to photograph. However, the thing that feels like it lights a fire inside me is writing. On the other hand the creation that I think will help me be understood is information structures. There is so much that I feel like I could enable the world to do, so many dreams I could enable to fruition with the tools I could create. I think that my brain is meant to complete this tool and to use this tool. To bridge the world's people, myself included, to the execution of their dream.   
The art that I seem to be able to receive most naturally seems to be conversation. Maybe second to architecture or fashion. I wonder what this means for my desire to be understood and how I go about it. I hear of artists taking the leap of faith, and going to the place where people of the same medium of voice go to communicate. To learn what they didn't know they didn't know. To sharpen iron with iron. Where is this place for me? How do I set out on a hero’s journeys so that I can return home.   
Perhaps I’m trying to write a story before it’s done being written, paying attention to the past and future to understand what is right in front of me. Perhaps I think too much about how I might communicate instead of what it is I wish to say. I guess I want to sing about hope. I want to be the person that I needed when I was younger, the message, the pillar, the mentor, the peace, the hero. I don’t want to leave that little boy, I don’t want him to feel like the world has forgotten, I don’t want him to want to disappear. He is valuable, he is bright and creative. I love him. I want him to feel loved.    
I want to someday write stories to have my many racing-spiraling thoughts adopted as guidees and inspirations. This is my tomorrow, but today I desire to climb a mountain to yell from higher heights. I want to struggle for greatness in building tools for every artist inside every person. I want to give power to the people and join a symphony of told truths.   
I am going to continue to struggle for this goal, I will bleed for this goal. I will die for this goal. I know this tool, that I bring closer and closer to reality, is one that will serve that little boy and all the children of the world. I will be the champion for the children and so I will be the champion for the artist and so I will be a champion for the world’s people that I give this tool to.  
I am impassioned by the truth of spoken word, by the hero’s journey of stories, my dreams of what could be. I want to enable my technology's capability for my car, for my website, for my invention process. These things serve me, but I find solace in knowing that this is okay. This is okay because I want to live in an overflowing manner, I want my vigor for life and something greater to become reachable for those around me. I want the power to seek greater truth in creation, and I want others to as well. I want to be renowned for bringing beauty and functionality to the world, and I want others to enjoy my tools and creation alike. I want to create spaces and movements with dreams that are the greater sum than the people that make them up. I want to create cyclical love by god's hand.   
It can be daunting for me to define what it is that I want, as questions arise of its permanence, of its all encompassing nature, of its rejection of adaptation. So let me say what it is that I want without meekness and with a power that I can hold on to in times of uncertainty. I want to  play an infinite game with this life of mine, an additive game, I want to create, to communicate.
