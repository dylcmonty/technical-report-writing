# Cross-domain data-interface work validates FND's technical wedge

## Claim

My current campaign data-interface consulting is not the target market, but it is relevant technical proof for FND.

The common problem across campaign operations and local agriculture is not the domain itself. It is the presence of fragmented operational data that must be normalized into useful, inspectable, decision-supporting structures.

## Reasoning

The agriculture product requires me to:

- ingest messy records
- normalize fields and identifiers
- preserve provenance
- surface structured operational data
- support coordination across multiple independent actors
- make domain-specific information usable for downstream workflows

The campaign data-interface work exercises the same category of capability, even though the end market is different. I treat it as paid technical validation, not as the business focus.
