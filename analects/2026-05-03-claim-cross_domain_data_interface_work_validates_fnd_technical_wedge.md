# Cross-domain data-interface work validates FND's technical wedge

## Claim

FND's current campaign data-interface consulting is not the target market, but it is relevant technical proof.

The common problem across campaign operations and local agriculture is not the domain itself. It is the presence of fragmented operational data that must be normalized into useful, inspectable, decision-supporting structures.

## Reasoning

The agriculture product requires FND to:

- ingest messy records;
- normalize fields and identifiers;
- preserve provenance;
- surface structured operational data;
- support coordination across multiple independent actors;
- make domain-specific information usable for downstream workflows.

The campaign data-interface work exercises the same category of capability, even though the end market is different.

## YC posture

In a YC application, this should be described as paid technical validation, not as the business focus.

Correct formulation:

> Current highest-paying work is data-interface consulting outside agriculture. It validates the technical pattern FND needs for agriculture: turning fragmented domain records into structured operational data. The target scalable business remains buyer-side local produce procurement.
