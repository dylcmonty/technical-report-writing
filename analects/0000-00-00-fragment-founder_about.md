## Fragment

I build software and data infrastructure for local food systems.

I am the founder of Fruitful Network Development, where I develop the open-source software that operates on my novel data framework to provide tools for farms and community food organizations: websites, inventory and ordering workflows, payments, operations systems, and data models that let producers publish current and expected supply without giving up localized impact or control.

My broader interest is making regional supply visible and actionable. I am especially focused on open standards, coordination systems, and pilotable infrastructure that can reduce waste, improve logistics, and make local sourcing more economically competitive.

Previously, I worked as a System Engineer at Stanley Black & Decker on prototype electrical systems, and as an Undergraduate Student Researcher at The University of Akron in human-computer interaction.

I am interested in connecting with researchers, founders, operators, and institutions working in agtech, supply chains, open-source software, logistics, and technical infrastructure.
