# Research Description

In less than 500 words, please describe your research project, including:
- Scientific question or problem being addressed
- Methodology and approach
- Expected outcomes and deliverables
- Timeline for completion

This project tests whether an open, semantics-aware data infrastructure can measurably improve the productivity and planning reliability of small diversified farms by reducing coordination uncertainty between production, inventory, and downstream demand. The
 scientific problem is that small farms often operate with fragmented records and incompatible tools, making it difficult to study or improve the relationship between planting plans, expected supply, sell-through certainty, waste, and buyer reliability at a
 systems level. The project’s methodology is to build and refine an interoperable agricultural data schema; deploy prototype tools with a small pilot group of Northeast Ohio farms and food-system partners; and collect baseline and pilot-period data on record-keeping
 burden, supply visibility, sell-through, shrink, response latency, and planning confidence. The research focus is not only software usability, but whether better structured and interoperable information changes measurable agricultural coordination outcomes
 under realistic operating constraints. Expected deliverables are: an open technical specification for the schema; a working pilot software prototype; a normalized multi-farm research dataset; a feasibility analysis of interoperability and productivity effects;
 and a report defining the conditions under which broader deployment is justified. The timeline is approximately eight months: months 1–2 for literature review and schema refinement, months 2–4 for prototype and data-pipeline implementation, months 4–7 for
 pilot onboarding and data collection, and months 7–8 for analysis, documentation, and publication-ready reporting.
