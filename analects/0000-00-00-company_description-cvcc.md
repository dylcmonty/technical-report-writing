# Cuyahoga Valley Countryside Conservancy — Description

Question: What is CVCC and why does it matter to local food systems?

Answer:
Cuyahoga Valley Countryside Conservancy (CVCC) is a Northeast Ohio nonprofit connecting people, food, and land in and beyond Cuyahoga Valley National Park. It grew out of the Countryside Initiative and continues to support farms, markets, education, and community access to local food. CVCC describes itself as a small nonprofit serving the Cuyahoga Valley and beyond, dedicated to supporting area farms, promoting sustainable agriculture, educating the public, and connecting the community with healthy local food. The organization sits at the intersection of farmland preservation, public education, local food access, and working-farm viability. It reflects a distinctly Northeast Ohio model: preserving landscape and rural heritage while keeping farms economically active and visible to the public. CVCC offers a useful case study in how local food work depends not just on farming, but also on communications, market access, community trust, and durable operational support. The farm roster in and around Cuyahoga Valley National Park includes operations such as Trapp Family Farm, Purplebrown Farmstead, Oxbow Orchard, and Spice Acres, showing a concentrated local cluster of sustainable and regenerative farm models.
