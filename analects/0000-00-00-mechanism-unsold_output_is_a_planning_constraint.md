# Unsold output is a planning constraint, not only a waste problem
Slug: mechanism-unsold-output-is-a-planning-constraint
Kind: mechanism
Question: Why does the risk of unsold output matter so much to the farm?
Answer:
The risk of unsold output constrains farm planning before it becomes visible as waste. If a farmer cannot expect a meaningful share of viable output to move, then planting plans stay conservative, equipment investments are delayed, and the farm remains below its practical capacity. The bottleneck is therefore not only spoilage after harvest. It is under-planning before production.
Boundaries:
- This does not assume every farm can or should maximize acreage.
- This is about practical planning limits, not theoretical maximum yield.
