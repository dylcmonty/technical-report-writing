# Cuyahoga Valley Countryside Conservancy — Description

## Retrieval Gist

What is CVCC and why does it matter to local food systems?

## Description
Cuyahoga Valley Countryside Conservancy (CVCC) is a Northeast Ohio nonprofit connecting people, food, and land in and beyond Cuyahoga Valley National Park. It grew out of the Countryside Initiative launched in 1999 to preserve agriculture in the park as a living, working landscape rather than a static historical display. The model matters because it is structural: long-horizon stewardship leases, nonprofit support, farmer recruitment, and recurring market interfaces give farms enough continuity to remain publicly visible and economically active. CVCC therefore sits at the intersection of farmland preservation, public education, local food access, and working-farm viability. It reflects a distinctly Northeast Ohio model: preserving landscape and rural heritage while keeping farms economically active and visible to the public. CVCC offers a useful case study in how local food work depends not just on farming, but also on tenure, market access, community trust, and durable operational support. The farm roster in and around Cuyahoga Valley National Park includes operations such as Trapp Family Farm, Purplebrown Farmstead, Oxbow Orchard, and Spice Acres, showing a concentrated local cluster of sustainable and regenerative farm models.
