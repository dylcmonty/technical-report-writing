# Pitch Statement — FND Core Purpose

Question: How does FND explain its purpose to a general audience in four minutes?

Answer:
The goal is simple: give small farms and food networks the tools to operate like the big players, without losing their independence. Most people want to buy local, but the system makes it hard. Small, sustainable farms heavily rely on hubs like CSAs and farmers markets, yet these organizers face logistical chaos. Managing supply from dozens of farms with no unified tools results in inefficiency, waste, and lost opportunity. The demand, the people, and the product are all here — it is the coordination that is broken. FND uses an open, modular framework that anyone can build on, adapt, and trust, without having to worry about how separate applications and websites communicate. They just do. We are starting in agriculture, but the same approach has potential to transform any community-focused industry. We do not want to own this system; we want to build it with the people who need it.

Market framing:
Family farms control most U.S. farmland. If local producers could close the 25% output-per-acre gap caused by inefficient distribution of perishable crops, an additional $70 billion in annual value could be realized nationally. In Ohio alone, this could recover an estimated $800 million per year. FND's revenue model is aligned with outcomes: the company only gets paid when its customers do better.

Boundaries:
- The market numbers ($70B, $800M) require source verification before use in formal proposals.
- "Operate like the big guys without losing independence" is a pitch framing; the technical implementation is more precisely described in the Mycite framework and ownership model snippets.
