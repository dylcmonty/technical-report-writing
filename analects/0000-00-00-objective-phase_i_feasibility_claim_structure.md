# Phase I Feasibility Claim Structure

Question: What structure must a Phase I feasibility claim take to be evaluable?

Answer:
A Phase I feasibility claim must identify one specific decision, one minimum demonstration, and one explicit failure condition. The decision is what USDA needs Phase I to enable: proceed to Phase II because X is feasible, or stop because it is not. The demonstration is the minimum experiment that can answer that decision within eight months — a prototype, dataset, and validation protocol, not a platform roadmap. The failure condition is what result would make the conclusion "not feasible." Without a named falsifier, the claim is not a feasibility claim. When those three elements are defined, the work plan becomes a set of tasks that make the test possible; the innovation section becomes the minimum novelty needed to pass; and the abstract collapses to a defensible length because it is anchored to a real test.

Boundaries:
- This is a discipline rule for structuring a proposal, not a market claim.
- It applies inside the SBIR context; other funding contexts may have different requirements.
