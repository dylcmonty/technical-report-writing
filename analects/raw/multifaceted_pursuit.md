One thing not on my resume, but of which I am certainly proud, is my work on my software project’s data schema. I’m proud of how it has evolved, as well as the multidisciplinary research I conducted to develop it. By that, I mean I was trying to learn what I didn’t know I didn’t know, all while developing the code and schema. I came to understand ideas such as the nature of NP-complete problems, ontology, and self-organizing systems; concepts I only discovered after talking to professors of speech pathology and reading any book that caught my interest, including those that seemed unrelated to my project, like Surreal Numbers by Donald Knuth or Invisible Cities by Italo Calvino.
I’m proud of this multifaceted pursuit because of a story I once heard about Galileo: he recognized what he saw through his telescope as mountains on the moon partly because of his time as a painter. He pursued what fascinated him and valued studiousness in creative endeavors, which gave him a unique perspective. While I don’t have a “mountain” I can point out for others (yet), I believe this same dedication to creativity and curiosity is what I hope to achieve one day with my project.
