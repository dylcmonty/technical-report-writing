# Transitional Feasibility — Two-Sided Viability

Question: What does Phase I need to demonstrate to show that local coordination is feasible?

Answer:
The Phase I investigation targets transitional feasibility: whether a coordination pathway exists in which farms get a net positive gain and food hubs or grocery buyers get a net-zero or net-positive gain, without undercutting either party. The research is not a claim that local agriculture immediately wins. It is a feasibility investigation of realizing unrealized value — determining whether, given current farm output and validated capacity bands, a coordination and routing model can compress overhead enough that both sides of the market improve. The farm side is measured by income volatility reduction or margin improvement. The buyer side is measured by delivered cost parity and service-level floor. The simulation uses observed production data and farmer-validated capacity bounds as inputs, not agronomic yield prediction. If there exists a plausible coordination scenario where both constraints are satisfied, Phase I has found evidence of a transitional path. If no such scenario exists within the data collected, Phase I has returned a meaningful negative result.

Boundaries:
- This is not a claim that the cyclical improvement loop is already initiated.
- The claim is that a pathway exists in the data; actual initiation depends on adoption, commitment, and Phase II deployment.
- This is not agronomic optimization — it uses observed output and capacity bands, not biophysical yield modeling.
