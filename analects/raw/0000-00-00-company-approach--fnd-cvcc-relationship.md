# FND and CVCC Relationship

Question: How should the relationship between FND and CVCC be accurately described?

Answer:
Fruitful Network Development supports CVCC with digital infrastructure and operational systems work beyond basic website service. FND's public model is open-source, no-vendor-lock-in, and ownership-retaining: clients keep control of domains, payment systems, and mailboxes. In this context, CVCC should be understood as part of the real-world environment in which FND is learning, building, and testing practical agricultural software and coordination infrastructure. CVCC's digital work is part of a broader effort to modernize local agricultural coordination without forcing farmers into closed software platforms. FND's role is not just to maintain a website, but to build practical operating infrastructure that can support communication, payments, recordkeeping, and eventually optional interoperability across local food networks. The partnership reflects a practical thesis: independent farms and farm-support organizations need modern digital infrastructure without vendor lock-in, loss of ownership, or pressure to centralize control.

Boundaries:
- Avoid implying that CVCC is a software company or that FND's future brokerage and API network are already deployed through CVCC unless separately announced.
- Current, public, organization-level facts should be sourced first from CVCC and National Park Service materials.
