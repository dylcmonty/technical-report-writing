# FND startup posture as food brokerage

FND is best represented in commercial settings as a technology-enabled food brokerage and coordination company for local produce.

The company helps grocery stores, food hubs, institutions, and other recurring food buyers source more usable local agricultural supply. Farmer-facing tools, web services, open-source infrastructure, and data conventions support that commercial posture by making farm output more visible, reliable, and coordinatable.

This positioning keeps the company legible as a business. The buyer-side problem is procurement reliability: local supply is fragmented, timing is inconsistent, inventory is difficult to see, and buyers often default to centralized distributors because they reduce transaction friction. FND addresses that failure by building the supply-side data participation needed for brokerage.

In this frame, farmers are essential participants and strategic beneficiaries, but grocers, food hubs, and recurring buyers are the clearer paying customers. Open-source farmer tools are not charity or the whole business category. They are a participation strategy that reduces adoption friction and builds trusted supply liquidity.

Compact formulation:

FND makes local produce sourcing reliable for grocers and food hubs by giving farmers tools that turn fragmented farm output into brokerable supply.
