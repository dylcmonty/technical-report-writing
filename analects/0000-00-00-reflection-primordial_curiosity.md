# Primordial Curiosity

## Retrieval Gist

Reflection on self-directed technical formation in which curiosity, not credentials, is the motive force behind learning hardware, software, and mechanical systems.

## Use When

Use when explaining the personal engine behind Dylan Montgomery's technical development, especially in founder narratives, reflective bios, or interviews about how he learns.

## Text

My background is a mix of self-motivated hardware and software projects. I have taught myself KiCad to prototype a handheld device, built a laminar flow hood out of curiosity, and introduced myself to C++ through Speeduino and real-time engine data. Every new problem I face, whether mechanical or computational, fuels a kind of primordial curiosity about the world and what might be created within it.

## Interpretive Note

This note describes motive and learning posture. It should not be used as a substitute for formal employment history or credentials.
