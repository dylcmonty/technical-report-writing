# FND Target Customer

## Retrieval Gist

Customer-definition fragment for FND that distinguishes the farmer-side operational users from the buyer-side network and procurement users.

## Use When

Use when describing who needs FND, who pays, and how the customer set expands from farm operations into network coordination.

## Fragment

Our primary customers are small and mid-sized producers and cooperatives who grow fruits, vegetables and specialty crops valued for their quality or sustainability. These farmers often sell through community-supported agriculture (CSA) programs, farmers' markets or regional distributors but lack integrated planning and inventory tools. FND replaces spreadsheets with modules for crop planning, harvest schedules and inventory tracking; producers pay a subscription to use these tools independently and can opt into network services when they need aggregated demand or logistics support. Because organic, regenerative and non-GMO producers already command price premiums, they are willing to invest in technology that helps document practices and connect to conscientious buyers.

Beyond individual farms, regional food hubs, cooperatives and certification bodies will use the network layer. Food hubs need real-time visibility into supply and demand to coordinate aggregated deliveries; cooperatives need shared analytics and compliance tools to enforce standards; certification agencies require verifiable provenance and policy pipelines to maintain trust. These stakeholders are prepared to pay transaction or service fees for network access because it streamlines their operations, reduces manual communication and gives them scalable market access without surrendering control. Eventually, wholesalers and institutional buyers could integrate with the platform through APIs or a buyer portal, broadening the customer base.
