# Local Agriculture Is Increasingly Inequitable to Work With and In

## Retrieval Gist

What is the central organizing problem in local agriculture?

## Claim
Increasingly, it is inequitable to work with and in local agriculture — which remains the central organizing force of rural communities. The constraint is not primarily a demand problem. People want local, sustainable food. The constraint is structural: small farms cannot plan confidently, cannot reliably supply buyers, and cannot compete on price with industrial alternatives — not because they are less capable as producers, but because the coordination infrastructure they operate within fails them. The income volatility that results is not incidental. It is the primary mechanism through which farms are kept below their productive capacity, communities lose their agricultural base, and local food systems remain fragile despite genuine consumer demand.
## Boundaries
- This is a problem framing claim, not a complete causal model.
- The inequity claim is specifically about the structural conditions of coordination and planning, not a general social justice claim.
