# Pitch Statement — FND Core Purpose

## Retrieval Gist

How does FND explain its purpose to a general audience in four minutes?

## Fragment
The goal is simple: give small farms and food networks the tools to operate like the big players without losing their independence. Most people want to buy local, but the system makes it hard. Small, sustainable farms rely on hubs like CSAs and farmers markets, yet these organizers face logistical chaos. Managing supply from dozens of farms with no unified tools results in inefficiency, waste, and lost opportunity. The demand, the people, and the product are all here. It is the coordination that is broken. I use an open, modular framework that people can build on, adapt, and trust without worrying about how separate applications and websites communicate. I am starting in agriculture, but I think the same approach can apply in other community-focused industries.
## Market Framing
Family farms control most U.S. farmland. If local producers could close the 25% output-per-acre gap caused by inefficient distribution of perishable crops, an additional $70 billion in annual value could be realized nationally. In Ohio alone, this could recover an estimated $800 million per year. My revenue model is aligned with outcomes: I only get paid when the work creates better operating results for the customer.
## Boundaries
- The market numbers ($70B, $800M) require source verification before use in formal proposals.
- "Operate like the big guys without losing independence" is a pitch framing; the technical implementation is more precisely described in the Mycite framework and ownership model snippets.
