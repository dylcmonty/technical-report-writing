# Demand certainty creates a reinforcement loop

## Retrieval Gist

What is the reinforcing cycle I keep pointing to?

## Claim
If a farm can expect viable output to sell within a usable time window, it can tolerate less planning risk. That increases usable volume and stabilizes cash flow. More stable cash flow supports reinvestment in storage, equipment, labor, and handling. Those improvements increase reliability, which then supports stronger future sell-through. The cycle compounds until it reaches the limits imposed by distance, product mix, handling capacity, and demand.
## Boundaries
- This is a reinforcing loop, not a promise of unlimited growth.
- The loop still depends on service radius, product variety, and buyer demand.
