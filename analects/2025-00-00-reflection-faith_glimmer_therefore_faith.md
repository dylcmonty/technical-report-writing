# Faith

## Retrieval Gist

Personal spiritual reflection that frames faith as hope-guided fidelity to a truth only partially seen.

## Use When

Use when retrieving personal writing about hope, responsibility, conviction, and the burden of following what feels true before it is legible to others.

## Text

People call it genius; others call it delusional tunnel vision.
- To me, it’s best explained as a glimmer.
- Imagine one person staring out into the dark woods. To them, it might seem obvious, there’s a glimmer, a faint starlight reflected off something unseen. And yet, they alone notice it. Others are confused, unable to see what they see. Maybe it’s only because they happen to be standing in just the right spot. But still, they choose to believe in the thing they see, even when the world does not recognize it.
- Some people may never see another’s glimmer. Yet they are inspired by their peer’s confidence to venture into the dark woods. 
- Others may baffled and solely fixated only by those that follow their vision and wonder: How can my peer believe there’s a path forward, free of obstruction? But their peer does not wonder. If they can see the glimmer, there is no question whether they’ll be able to follow it. To them, why would they focus on anything else? Amidst mundanity, the glimmer is the only thing worth seeing; ignoring it would be what’s truly baffling.
- If we try to follow another person’s truth without making it our own, we cannot be truly guided by it. We will question it, stumble, and miss the flickering calls meant only for the one who sees.
- I believe truth exists. It is not the glimmer itself, but the nature of the world that allows the glimmer to be. I believe we are given life to foster belief in that truth, a belief in the possibility of a path forward.
- More than that, we bear the weight of responsibility. Once that accidental glimmer catches our eye, we must choose to follow it.
- It is difficult. We must try to describe this glimmer, even though we can never fully show it to another. We must encourage others to take our hand through the uncertainty; until they, too, find their own beacon.
- We take on this task not to be correct, but to offer respect to this correctness. For without faith, there is only hopelessness.
- I am a warrior, not against or for a particular path, though that is the journey I am on. I am not a warrior against the fearful or the faithless; they are my brothers and sisters, as I am sometimes one of them, losing sight of hope.
- I am a warrior for hope. I can only believe in good and better because of my acquaintance with God, perhaps from afar, perhaps only through reflection of where I stand. But I know God is there because of my belief. I know there is truth because I have hope.

## Interpretive Note

This is a personal spiritual reflection, not a doctrinal summary or an empirical claim.
