# Coordinated Local Agriculture Pathway

## Retrieval Gist

Why is the transition pathway more important than claiming an end-state outcome?

## Claim
The transition pathway matters because local agriculture is often stuck in a chicken-and-egg condition: farms cannot scale without coordination, and coordination is difficult without reliable scale. A credible pathway shows how coordination starts under bounded conditions, produces initial reliability, and then creates compounding improvements in planning, pricing stability, and reinvestment. The practical goal is not to claim full replacement of industrial supply; it is to show a repeatable route to a stable local niche that is economically durable.
## Boundaries
- This does not claim that local agriculture can or should replace all industrial supply.
- This does not claim the end-state is guaranteed without bounded transition conditions.
