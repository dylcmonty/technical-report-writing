# Market Food Hubs Help But Do Not Remove Structural Pressures

Food hubs help, but they do not remove the structural pressures
Food hubs matter because they reduce transaction friction through aggregation, shared handling, and repeatable buyer relationships. But they do not erase pricing pressure, seasonality, buyer standards, or the need to balance supply and demand. They show that infrastructure matters, but they also show that partial aggregation alone does not fully solve the structural disadvantage.
Boundaries:
- This is not an anti-food-hub claim.
- This does not treat existing hubs as failures.
