# Terry Scholarship Leadership and Involvement

My leadership has mostly come through responsibility rather than formal title. I have led by building, organizing, mentoring, and continuing work through uncertain conditions.

As the founder and developer of Fruitful Network Development LLC, I have been responsible for research design, software development, data schema architecture, pilot coordination, and communication with farms and community organizations. That work has required me to speak with business owners, farmers, market organizers, and technical partners; identify operational problems; and translate those problems into practical software and infrastructure work. It has also required persistence. I have had to self-teach tools, revise assumptions, restart parts of the business model, and keep the work grounded in actual stakeholder needs rather than abstract ideas.

My involvement in the University of Akron Research Foundation I-Corps program was another important leadership experience. Through that process, I conducted extensive customer discovery across the agriculture sector and learned how to test whether an idea addresses a real problem. That experience strengthened my ability to listen, adapt, and make decisions based on evidence. It also helped me understand that leadership in technical work is not just presenting a vision; it is being willing to revise the vision when reality shows that a better path is needed.

I have also volunteered as a mentor with Big Brothers Big Sisters since February 2022. In that role, I meet regularly with my mentee, help coordinate activities, and support academic and personal growth. That experience has taught me a different form of leadership: consistency, patience, and trust. It has reinforced that helping someone grow is not only about giving advice. It is about showing up repeatedly and helping them see their own potential more clearly.

Across school, business, and service, my involvement has followed the same pattern. I look for places where a real constraint exists, then work to understand it and build something useful in response. Whether I am working with farmers, mentoring a younger student, or developing software, I try to lead by taking responsibility for the gap between an idea and its execution.
