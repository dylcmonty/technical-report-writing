# Open source supports trust and network scale

Open-source software is strategically useful for FND because local agricultural coordination requires trust, adoption, and participation across many independent actors.

A closed platform can simplify coordination by forcing everyone into one convention, but that same control can discourage participation. Farms, food hubs, and local organizations may avoid tools that seem to capture their data, constrain their autonomy, or make them dependent on a single vendor.

Open-source farmer tools lower adoption friction by making the infrastructure more inspectable, reusable, and adaptable. This does not eliminate the need for a business model. It shifts monetization toward buyer-side procurement, brokerage, services, implementation, analytics, and transaction coordination.

In this model, openness is not a side principle. It is part of the market-entry strategy.
