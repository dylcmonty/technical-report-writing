# Farmer tools as the supply-network wedge

FND uses farmer-facing tools as the adoption wedge for a larger local-produce coordination business.

Farms and agricultural organizations often need immediate operational support: websites, hosting, forms, account access, inventory structures, payment integrations, records, and basic workflow tools. Those needs create a low-friction entry point because they solve problems before asking farms to participate in a new procurement system.

The wedge becomes strategically valuable when ordinary operational data can be structured into supply visibility. A farm profile, crop plan, harvest window, inventory entry, price band, location, certification, or order record can become part of a brokerable supply picture. That supply picture is what buyers and hubs need in order to source locally with less uncertainty.

The farmer-side tools should therefore be free or low-cost where possible. Their purpose is to make participation rational for farms, increase trust, and build the supply network that makes buyer-side brokerage valuable.
