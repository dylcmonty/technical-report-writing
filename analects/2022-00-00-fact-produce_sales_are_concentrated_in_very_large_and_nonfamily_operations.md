# Produce sales are concentrated in very large and nonfamily operations

## Retrieval Gist

What national produce fact is useful when correcting broad claims about small-farm dominance?

## Fact

Using 2022 Census farm-typology proxies for vegetables and berries, produce sales were heavily concentrated in very large family and nonfamily operations. In the vegetable-group proxy, small family farms under $350,000 GCFI accounted for about 4.1 percent of sales, while very large family and nonfamily farms accounted for about 76.8 percent combined. The same evidence pack also showed higher gross sales per acre for very large family and nonfamily operations than for small-family categories in those national proxies.

## Boundaries

- This uses national proxy categories, including a vegetable group that also contains melons, potatoes, and sweet potatoes.
- It is a concentration fact, not a claim that small farms are unimportant locally or cannot be productive in specific niches.
