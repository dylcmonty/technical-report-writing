# Trapp Family Farm — Description

Question: What is Trapp Family Farm and why is it significant?

Answer:
Trapp Family Farm is a sustainable mixed crop-and-livestock family farm in Peninsula, Ohio, situated within the Cuyahoga Valley farm network. Since 2012 the farm has used draft horses for power and sold seasonal eggs, produce, and meat on site, emphasizing healthy soil, healthy animals, and a resilient local community. The farm is a strong counterexample to the assumption that agricultural progress must look industrial. Its draft-horse model gives it narrative distinction, but the deeper significance is operational: it shows how older and newer forms of efficiency can coexist. Trapp Family Farm is notable not only as a producer but as a living demonstration of labor, stewardship, and scale-conscious independence. Fruitful Network Development supports the farm with digital infrastructure and farm-support systems beyond commodity website service. The pairing is intentional: the farm can remain fully itself while gaining better infrastructure. Together, the farm and the technology partner create a more compelling story than either one alone — this is not software replacing farming, but software built around the realities of farming.
