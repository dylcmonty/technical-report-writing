# Local-food scaling requires intermediary and institutional channels

## Retrieval Gist

What does the 2020 local-food sales mix imply about where local food actually scales?

## Claim

The 2020 local-food sales picture supports a procurement-first reading of the market. Direct farm sales of local edible food totaled about $9.0 billion, and institutions plus intermediaries accounted for 46 percent of those sales, compared with 33 percent through direct-to-consumer channels. That means scaling local food is not mainly a story about consumer preference alone. It depends on meeting the operational requirements of buyers, aggregators, and institutions.

## Boundaries

- This does not deny the importance of CSAs, farmers markets, or other direct channels.
- It treats channel mix as a market-structure signal, not as proof that every intermediary model is healthy.
