# FND Strategic Thesis

Question: What is FND's core position on why local agriculture underperforms, and what is the company's answer to it?

Answer:
FND's thesis is that local agriculture is not mainly constrained by demand. It is constrained by fragmented operations, weak supply visibility, procurement friction, and poor interoperability across the systems that farms, markets, and buyers already use. The position is not that local agriculture needs better branding alone. It is that local agriculture needs better coordination, cleaner interfaces, and shared machine-readable visibility of supply, demand, timing, perishability, and commitments. Producers should be able to offer in-stock or expected supply from the same systems used to manage websites, inventory, POS, taxes, planning, and events. The intended result is dependable local provisioning that improves income stability, reduces waste, and preserves local control rather than deepening vendor lock-in.

Boundaries:
- This thesis frames the long-range strategic direction; the current business is an incremental wedge into that direction through practical farm services.
- The coordination layer is not yet fully deployed; it is being validated through SBIR Phase I work.
