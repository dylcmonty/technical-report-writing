# Data forfeiture blocks local agriculture tech adoption

Question: What is the current problem with local-agriculture technology solutions from a data perspective?

Answer:

The data umbrella term or data forfeiture idea is that there is no real immediate gain to be had my farmers for offering up their data or participation with tech solutions.

More over, there is an obvious blockade to progress and growth as a profit model develops on the assumption that they alone are capable of developing all the necessary software, tools, and library recurred to support a networked system of data, assuming that their convention for the expected data has no draw backs or retraint that prevents even open source momentum from supporting outside cooperation. Additionally, this approach does not posture its self to benefit from compounding development.

FND supports the development of the Mycite portal and its host of tools, being open source and free. Additionally, the portal its self is geared toward the hosting machines autonomy of operation, controlled communication, and usage of data conventions, there in, only possible view the usage of localized MOS database handling.

This is a pivotal part of FND's USDA proposal for investigation into feasibility of aiding in Rural Communities' via the enhancement of equability to work with and and in local agriculture.

Part of that application requires the ability to show how a solution of small businesses novel technology will go on to impact the greater issue at hand. In FND's case, the open source nature is meant to enable proliferation and documentation of developmental adoption for the solutions ability to reach all walks of life in the programs pursuit.

FND tackles both these points by supporting the development of open source software and tool that enable a universal language of information and convention, allowing for tool that improve upon the current use of excel or even pen and paper. Further more, the data forfeiture that is absolutely necessary for centralized coordination approaches is a non sequitur for FND's use of the Mycelial Ontological Schema (MOS).
