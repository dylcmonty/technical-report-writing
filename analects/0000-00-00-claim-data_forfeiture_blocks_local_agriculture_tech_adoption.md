# Data forfeiture blocks local agriculture tech adoption

## Retrieval Gist

What is the current problem with local-agriculture technology solutions from a data perspective?

## Claim
I do not think farmers have a good immediate reason to surrender their data or participation to most local-agriculture technology products.

When a business model assumes one platform must own the tools, data conventions, and coordination surface, adoption slows down. Farmers are asked to give up information before they receive enough day-to-day value in return, and outside developers have little room to compound on the system.

I am building FND and Mycite around the opposite assumption: open-source tools, local operational control, and shared conventions that let participation compound instead of forcing data forfeiture up front. That matters to my USDA framing because the public-benefit argument depends on a model that can spread without requiring every farm to surrender ownership to one centralized software party.
