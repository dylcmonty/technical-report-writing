# Unrealized Farm Value Through Planning Confidence

Question: What does unrealized value mean in the local farm context?

Answer:
Unrealized value is the gap between what a farm could produce and what it can responsibly plan to produce under uncertain sales conditions. Many farms are constrained less by land capability than by fear of growing into unsold inventory. When coordination improves demand visibility and sell-through confidence, farms can utilize more of their practical capacity, reduce waste, and convert dormant capability into realized economic value.

Boundaries:
- This does not claim every farm has identical latent capacity.
- This does not claim planning confidence alone solves all cost pressures.
