# Driven Approach

## Retrieval Gist

What kind of work motivates me, and why?

## Text
I am driven to build solutions that enable people by lowering barriers to innovation and coordination. My path is hands-on and self-directed: I taught myself KiCad to design a single-handed device inspired by historical instruments, built a laminar flow hood through curiosity about mycology, and entered software through C++ work with Speeduino and real-time engine data. That pattern continues in my agricultural work, where conversations with farmers, market organizers, and community leaders keep the work grounded in operational reality. The throughline is practical creativity: take a real constraint, build toward it iteratively, and keep the solution usable by the people it is meant to serve.
## Boundaries
- This is a motivation and working-style statement, not a claim of complete domain mastery.
- This does not claim that curiosity alone is sufficient without disciplined execution.
