# Transparency can improve efficiency while reducing operational equitability

## Retrieval Gist

Why can a more transparent market still become harder for ordinary participants to operate in?

## Claim

Better transparency reduces mismatch, lowers planning uncertainty, and makes prices and availability more actionable. But it also increases the strategic value of the market itself. That attracts control points, higher fixed costs, and rule-setting capture. A transparent market can therefore become more efficient while becoming less equitable to operate in. The design implication is that transparency helps ordinary participants only if access symmetry, low participation cost, and interoperable interfaces are preserved.

## Boundaries

- This does not argue for opacity as a solution.
- It does not claim every transparent market becomes captured to the same degree.
