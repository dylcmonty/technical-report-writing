# Team Vision Execution Qualifications

Why is your team the right group to execute on this vision? (Highlight experience, passion, skills, Max 100 words)
As a Computer Engineering and Applied Mathematics senior at the University of Akron, I’ve spent the past four years building, testing, and refining this idea from hypothesis to working framework. I've taken engineering far beyond learning in university courses by following what interests me, and then asking where my innovative ideas meet a demand. I put ideas into action, adapting through failures and learning directly from industry professionals. My passion for community-focused technology and my ability to self-teach and execute on complex systems makes me ready to lead Fruitful Network Development to success.
Having placed 3rd in the University of Akron’s regional I-corp pitch Competition, I’ve done hundreds of hours of market research and customer discovery talking to the agriculture industry. Half way though the process I actually restarted because I needed to change my business model to optimize how my company addresses the problems the technology addresses. Having placed in 3rd place, I was unable to advance to larger regional competitions as I was a solo community team, despite being at the university of akron, my technology was not associated so I had to find a way to advance my mission. I created my company and began coordinating with my local agriculture network that I had created to start prototyping the technologies application and building the early adopter that I will be abe to deploy with. I have written letters of impact for the work that I do for local farms, as well as charities like the Cuyahoga Valley Countryside Conservancy and their participant farmstead in the Cuyahoga Valley National Park.
I have also worked alongside the Ohio State Extension program’s North East Ohio Agricultural Innovation Office to help farmers and farming organizations. I frequently speak with the local farmers burrow and farmer’s union chapters. I am looking to connect our communities with themselves to have a better federated system of cooperation. That cooperation is as applicable to technology as it is to the people that are given those tools.
