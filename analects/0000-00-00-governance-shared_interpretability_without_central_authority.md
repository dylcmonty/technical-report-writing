# Shared interpretability is enough; full central authority is not required
Slug: governance-shared-interpretability-without-central-authority
Kind: architecture
Question: Why do I keep resisting a centralized model?
Answer:
A centralized authority can impose interoperability by force, but that also makes coordination dependent on one controlling system. My position is that local actors need enough shared interpretability to coordinate without surrendering autonomy. The requirement is not absolute trustlessness in the abstract. It is practical coordination without mandatory central ownership of the rules.
Boundaries:
- This is a governance position tied to implementation.
- It should not be inflated into unnecessary philosophical claims in the proposal body.
