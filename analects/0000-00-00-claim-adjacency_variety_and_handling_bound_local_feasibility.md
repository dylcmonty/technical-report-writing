# Adjacency, variety, and handling bound local feasibility

## Retrieval Gist

What hard conditions shape whether coordinated local sourcing is actually viable?

## Claim

The local feasibility window expands where farms and buyers are geographically adjacent, the product basket is broad enough to cover meaningful demand, and handling capacity is adequate for perishability. It contracts when routes are long, volumes are too fragmented, or cold-chain, wash-pack, labor, or scheduling bottlenecks erase the advantage of proximity. Local coordination is therefore not a universal win condition. It is a bounded operating regime shaped by radius, variety, and execution capacity.

## Boundaries

- This does not argue against local sourcing in principle.
- It does not claim software alone can remove physical limits imposed by time, distance, or handling.
