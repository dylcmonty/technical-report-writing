# Gray

Remembering the past self, full of faults, can
feel like a dream. A dream in which I was
a beast. A moment I remember living, but
one I was not conscious within. There
are memories of what my senses observed,
but there is no recollection of being
that beast and reflecting as that beast.

That kind of dream, you wake up from. Then
give thanks for the dream, because it was
only a dream. You revere the sophisticated
awareness that exists in your present
metacognition. Before, the dream was reality.
Merely a beast with a limited sense of reality:
to survive, to not feel bad, and to feel good.

You are better now.
You see beyond the view of that limited existence.
But how can I know that I have awakened?
How can I know that I am not still yet
to become aware of what I am not,
or what I do not live in knowing?

What am I missing?
What injustices do I see through?
What harm do I cause?
What stagnation do I cling to?

If I live to exist in the mind of others,
if the impression others hold of my self
and actions is allowed to make me what I am,
then I become the apotheosis of an object.

And if it is my intention that I consider law,
that I consider truth, as reality,
then it is everything outside myself
that I objectify with the disposition
of a lonely god.

I may be a beast and I may not,
but I refuse death in unchange
as an object,
and I refuse death in absolute righteousness
as a lonely god.

I choose intention.

This reality is not gray,
and it is not black and white.
It is black, and it is white,
not a mosaic, but everything all at once.

I choose intent.

To say my manner of thought is correct,
or to say my action is just,
is to choose death in certainty.

And if I surmise these as wrong,
then I choose death as an object,
and reject the ever-present reality
that the product of all that was,
once was,
and is what is now.

I have intention,
and I am because of what was.

I eat from the tree of life,
and I hope to ever wake from my dreams.

~2022
