# Local agriculture is constrained more by structure than by indifference
Slug: problem-local-agriculture-is-constrained-by-structure
Kind: problem
Question: What broader position does the project take on the local food economy?
Answer:
My broader position is that local agriculture is constrained more by structural economic and logistical conditions than by indifference from consumers. The surrounding market rewards standardized procurement, coordination efficiency, and post-farm services. That is why local preference can remain real while local agriculture stays economically disadvantaged.
Boundaries:
- This does not deny the role of price sensitivity.
- It frames price sensitivity as part of the structure, not a separate rebuttal.
