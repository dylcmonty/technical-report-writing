# Business model

FND’s business model should monetize buyer-side coordination and brokerage while keeping farmer participation low-friction.

The first revenue source is managed web and operations services for farms and agricultural organizations. This creates relationships and supply-side participation.

The scalable revenue model is buyer-side: grocers, food hubs, institutions, and other recurring local-food buyers pay for procurement coordination, brokerage, logistics coordination, and transaction execution. The company can charge through monthly buyer contracts, brokerage margins, transaction fees, or procurement-service fees.

A practical sequence is:

1. free or low-cost farmer tools to build supply participation;
2. paid buyer accounts for supply visibility and procurement coordination;
3. transaction revenue as FND helps route actual local produce purchases.
