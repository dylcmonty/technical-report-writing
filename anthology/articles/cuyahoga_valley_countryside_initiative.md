# Cuyahoga Valley National Park — Countryside Initiative

## Overview
The **Countryside Initiative** is a program in Cuyahoga Valley National Park (CVNP) designed to preserve agriculture as a living, working landscape rather than a static historic display. It began in **1999** and focuses on rehabilitating historic farmsteads while keeping them active under stewardship-aligned practices.

## Program mechanism: long-term leases and stewardship
The program’s core structure is institutional:

- Park-owned farmsteads are operated under **long-term leases** (a commonly referenced lease template specifies a **60-year term**).
- A nonprofit partner supports elements such as technical assistance, farmer recruitment, and program execution.

This approach emphasizes continuity: long lease terms can support long-horizon farm planning, infrastructure repair, and stewardship practices that are not easily justified under short tenancy.

## Farmers markets as a public interface
Within the broader program ecosystem, farmers markets function as a stable point of sale and community interface—an accessible, repeated venue that can lower market-entry barriers for smaller producers and build demand habits.

### Cuyahoga Valley Farmers Market
- The organization states it registered as a **501(c)(3) in 2022** to carry forward the Countryside Market tradition.
- It operates seasonal market schedules (Howe Meadow / Old Trail School).

## Notable public reporting
Public reporting describes nonprofit leadership transitions after founder Darwin Kelsey’s death (2016), including Tracy Emrick’s role as acting director prior to becoming executive director.
