# Agriculture as the Root of Local Community

Question: Why does local agriculture carry significance beyond food production?

Answer:
In a modern era where communities are losing cohesion, agriculture remains the deep-seated root of local economies. Organizations like CVCC recognize this and actively support working farmland as a form of community infrastructure, not just crop production. Agriculture, when supported, becomes a beacon of regional resilience. The farms in and around Cuyahoga Valley National Park are not isolated operations; they participate in a broader pattern where sustainable farming, regional food markets, and civic institutions are mutually reinforcing. That pattern is fragile. When working farms disappear, what goes with them is not just land use but the organizing structure of local life.

Boundaries:
- This is a framing claim, not a data-backed economic assertion.
- Use this for context-setting and mission framing, not as quantitative evidence.
