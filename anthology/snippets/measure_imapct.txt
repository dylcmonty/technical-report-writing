I think the impact that matters, without adopting a crystalized and stringent approach, is one that is two fold. 
The first being intentionality, but also this is a metric that can’t be measured. So even though forms of intentionality might not be obvious to different types of people I think that we can still sense it. 
I think someone said once that “we are all born, but at some point we choose to be human”, that can be dangerous, because then we might find ourselves saying that others are not human, however it still true. 
The second is living our lives in a way that almost rewards that form of living life. Seeking out people, missions, ect. that we believe to embody that intentionality.
I think that part of this is devoting our lives to something we see is missing in the world. This especially means that we do not take on the task of saving the world. It's easy to fall into a pit of nihilism if you do, how could someone ever save the world. However, if we have hope that there is goodness and that people other than ourselves have picked up that torch to support goodness in the fashion that they are called to, then we can rest easy that truth and goodness will prevail. We must give up in trying to control the world, and pursue goodness despite our enlightenment to our ignorance.
