# Instructions

## How to Enter and Publish Expected Produce Availability in Fruitful Network Development's Farm Operations Platform

### Overview

These instructions explain how a farm operator can enter expected produce availability into Fruitful Network Development's farm operations platform and then publish selected availability information for outside coordination. The purpose of this task is to let a farm maintain its own internal records while also making relevant supply information visible to approved external users such as buyers, food hubs, or regional partners.

These instructions are written for a general farm user who is new to the platform.

### What These Instructions Cover

This document explains how to:

1. log in to the platform
2. open the inventory and availability area
3. create a new produce entry
4. enter expected timing and quantity information
5. review the entry
6. publish selected availability data

### What These Instructions Do Not Cover

These instructions do not explain:

- how to create a new farm account
- how to manage billing or subscription settings
- how to edit user permissions for other staff
- how to integrate the platform with a separate point-of-sale system

### Before You Begin

Before starting, make sure you have the following information available:

- your username and password
- the name of the crop or product you want to enter
- the expected quantity or quantity range
- the expected harvest or availability window
- any notes about packaging, pickup, delivery, or limitations

### NOTE

If you are not sure of the exact quantity, enter a realistic quantity range rather than a precise number that may later need major correction.

### CAUTION

Do not publish private farm notes, internal cost information, or customer-specific pricing in the public availability field. Only publish information that is intended for outside coordination.

---

## Instructions

### 1. Log in to the platform

1. Open the farm operations platform in your browser.
2. Enter your username in the username field.
3. Enter your password in the password field.
4. Select **Log In**.

### 2. Open the inventory and availability area

1. From the main dashboard, locate the navigation menu.
2. Select **Inventory**.
3. In the inventory area, select **Expected Availability**.

### 3. Start a new availability entry

1. Select **Add New Entry**.
2. In the product name field, type the name of the produce item.
3. In the crop class or category field, select the correct category if one is available.

### 4. Enter the expected quantity

1. Locate the quantity section.
2. Enter the expected available amount.
3. If your expected amount is uncertain, enter a low estimate and a high estimate if the platform allows a range.
4. Select the correct unit, such as pounds, cases, bunches, or another listed unit.

### 5. Enter the expected timing

1. Locate the availability window section.
2. Enter the expected start date.
3. Enter the expected end date.
4. If the product will become available in stages, add separate date ranges if needed.

### 6. Enter handling and coordination notes

1. In the notes field, enter only information relevant to coordination.
2. Include details such as:
   - pickup only
   - delivery possible within a limited radius
   - minimum order size
   - packaging or container information
3. Do not enter confidential internal notes in this field if the field may be shared externally.

### 7. Review the entry before saving

1. Read through the product name, quantity, unit, and timing fields.
2. Make sure dates are correct and in the proper order.
3. Confirm that any coordination notes are accurate and intended for outside use.
4. Check that the entry does not contain private internal business information.

### 8. Save the entry

1. Select **Save Draft** if you want to keep the entry for internal use only.
2. Select **Save** if you are ready to keep the entry in the system.

### 9. Publish the availability entry

1. Open the saved entry.
2. Locate the sharing or publishing section.
3. Review which fields will be visible externally.
4. Select the appropriate audience, such as:
   - internal only
   - approved buyer network
   - regional coordination layer
5. Select **Publish**.

### 10. Confirm publication status

1. After publishing, check the status label on the entry.
2. Make sure it now shows the correct status, such as **Published** or **Shared**.
3. If the entry still shows **Draft**, repeat the publishing step and confirm that the correct audience was selected.

## Troubleshooting

### Problem: The entry will not save
- Make sure all required fields are completed.
- Check for missing quantity or date information.
- Confirm that the start date is not later than the end date.

### Problem: The wrong information is visible externally
- Open the entry again.
- Return to the sharing or publishing section.
- Review which fields are visible.
- Remove any field that should remain internal.
- Save and republish the corrected version.

### Problem: The quantity changed after publication
- Open the existing entry.
- Update the quantity field or quantity range.
- Save the change.
- Republish the entry so outside users see the updated information.

## Conclusion

By completing these steps, a farm operator can enter expected produce availability into the platform for internal record use and then publish selected information for outside coordination. This supports two connected goals: better internal farm organization and clearer external visibility into available local supply.
