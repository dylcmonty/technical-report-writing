# Cyclical Reinvestment Loop

Question: How does coordination produce compounding gains in local agriculture?

Answer:
When farms can expect sales for viable output, overproduction risk falls and planning becomes less defensive. That de-risking allows reinvestment into infrastructure, equipment, and operational upgrades, which improves reliability and lowers avoidable overhead. Higher reliability then increases buyer confidence and repeat demand, reinforcing planning confidence again. The loop is: reduced uncertainty -> reinvestment -> improved capacity and reliability -> stronger demand match -> further uncertainty reduction.

Boundaries:
- This does not claim infinite growth; the loop plateaus under real constraints such as geography and product variety.
- This does not claim gains occur without coordination quality and execution discipline.
