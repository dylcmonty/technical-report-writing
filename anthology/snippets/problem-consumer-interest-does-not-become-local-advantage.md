# Consumer interest does not become local advantage on its own
Slug: problem-consumer-interest-does-not-become-local-advantage
Kind: problem
Question: Why does consumer interest in local food not automatically create strong outcomes for local farms?
Answer:
Consumer interest by itself does not make local agriculture competitive. Local farms still have to move through procurement, scheduling, handling, delivery, compliance, and pricing systems that favor low-friction supply. A product can be preferred in principle and still remain disadvantaged in practice if it is harder to source reliably.
Boundaries:
- This does not claim demand is absent.
- This does not claim consumer values are unimportant.
