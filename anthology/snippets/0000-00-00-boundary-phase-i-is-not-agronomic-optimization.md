# Phase I Is Not Agronomic Optimization

Question: What does Phase I explicitly not include, and why does that boundary matter?

Answer:
Phase I does not attempt agronomic yield optimization. The simulation uses current farm output data obtained through farmer interviews and capacity bounds derived from acreage and farmer-validated planning assumptions. It does not include biophysical yield models, crop response functions, weather prediction curves, or generalized agronomic optimization. The capacity bound is treated as a band — a low, typical, and high range validated by the farmer — not a point estimate derived from a simulation. This matters because agronomic optimization is a large, under-constrained research program that cannot be completed or validated within an eight-month Phase I budget. Staying within the coordination and logistics feasibility scope keeps the claim defensible and the experiment completable.

Boundaries:
- Weather may be incorporated as a scenario variable or a range factor, not as a yield prediction model.
- This boundary is a scope discipline, not a claim that agronomic considerations are irrelevant to the broader project.
