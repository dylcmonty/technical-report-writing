# Coordination can compress overhead without centralizing ownership
Slug: mechanism-coordination-can-compress-overhead
Kind: mechanism
Question: How does coordination create economic improvement?
Answer:
Better coordination can lower overhead by reducing fragmented routing, repeated administrative work, partial loads, and mismatch between what is produced and what buyers are prepared to purchase. The claim is not that local food wins simply because it is local. The claim is that better coordination can let geographic proximity and lower waste begin to matter economically.
Boundaries:
- This does not claim every local route is cheaper than every conventional route.
- This does not eliminate compliance or buyer standards.
