# Bio Variants

## Short

I am a builder drawn to hard problems, local systems, and people on a mission. My work ranges from data schema design and ETL automation to hardware prototyping and engine-control experimentation, and I am most motivated when I am learning my way through an unfamiliar technical problem.

## Medium

I am a self-directed builder interested in the point where creativity and practicality meet. I have worked on software schema design, ETL processes for Medicare and Medicaid datasets, hardware prototyping in KiCad and SolidWorks, and Speeduino-based engine-control work. I spend a lot of time talking with small business owners, farmers, and community organizers because I want to understand technical needs as they are actually lived. When there is a problem in front of me, I find a bottomless source of motivation.

## Long

I am motivated by the desire to build things that make people more capable. That has led me across a wide range of technical domains, from software data schema design and ETL automation to hardware prototyping, battery-management considerations, and open-source engine-control systems. I tend to enter unfamiliar areas by learning what I do not yet know how to ask, which is why my work often pulls in ideas from ontology, self-organizing systems, and other fields that initially seem unrelated. I am also shaped by direct conversations with small business owners, farmers, market organizers, and community leaders, because I want technical work to stay accountable to real constraints and real needs. I am most at home in environments built by creative, smart people on a mission to solve problems.
